# Player Turns
After `‚òÄÔ∏è Daybreak`, each Player takes his or her turn in a *counter clockwise* sequence, starting with the `Anchor`, as determined in the **Board Setup**.

## Hero [ üßô‚Äç - `üé≤ 2d6 +6` ]
In `Battle`, the `üßô‚Äç Hero` is a unit itself with a roll of `üé≤ 2d6 +6`.  If the `üßô‚Äç Hero` falls in `Battle` (this is called `FAINTING`), its `Army` can still fight and if successful, the `üßô‚Äç Hero` will not die.

## Hero Movement [ üêé ]
Each `üßô‚Äç Hero` is able to `MOVE` up to `üé≤ 1d12 +12` Tiles.  A `üßô‚Äç Hero` may **only** expend movement from its own movement roll, and can voluntarily end its own movement for that turn at any point.

> **ELI5**
> 
> - *A `üßô‚Äç Hero` can't "gift" another `üßô‚Äç Hero` movement*
> - *A `üßô‚Äç Hero` does not have to use all of its movement*

A `üßô‚Äç Hero` may move on any **unoccupied** Tile for `üêé(-1)`.

If a Tile has multiple Tokens on it, then the Player must interact with ***each*** Token at an *additional* expense of `üêé(-1)` according to *Token Priority* (cf. **"Multiply-Occupied Tiles"**).

If the Player or `Ally` owns a `üè∞ Settlement` or `‚öíÔ∏è Mine`, then **all** `Alliance Heroes` can treat that Tile *as if* it were unoccupied.

---
- **`üí° REMINDER`** *`üßü Monster`, `‚öíÔ∏èÔ∏è Mines`, and `üè∞ Settlements` "occupy" an entire Tile and as such, only cost `üêé(-1)` total (i.e. Max `üêé(-1)` per Tile)*
- **`üí° REMINDER`** *`üéÅ Items` and `üí∞ Resources` cost ***an additional*** `üêé(-1)` per Token on that Tile (i.e. Max `üêé(-1) + üêé(-1 * Token Count)` per Tile)*
---

## Hero Death
In **any and all** cases where a `üßô‚Äç Hero` dies, **all** `üéÅ Items` on that `üßô‚Äç Hero` are dropped at the Tile on which the `üßô‚Äç Hero` died.

> **[ Special Case ]** If the `üßô‚Äç Hero` dies at a `üè∞ Settlement`, the `üéÅ Items` are instead placed within the `Armory` of that `üè∞ Settlement` and normal `Armory` rules apply.

If the `üßô‚Äç Hero` had any `Mercenaries [ ü§∫ ]` still alive within its command at the time of death (for any reason), then `1` `üßü Monster` Token is placed on the Tile, as well.

> **TIP:** Strategically retreating from a fight with a "Pawn Hero" can force your enemy to deal with a `üßü Monster` Token on his or her `üè∞ Settlement`.  Be careful, though, as a Player is rewarded for killing `üßü Monsters` and also has a `üé≤ 1dP` roll chance to acquire those `üßü Monsters`.

---
- **`üí° REMINDER`** *A `üßô‚Äç Hero` death at a `üè∞ Settlement` causes the `üéÅ Items` **not to be** rerolled, as the cards instead transfer from the `üßô‚Äç Hero` directly to the `Armory` of the `üè∞ Settlement`.*
- **`üí° REMINDER`** *In the case of a `üßô‚Äç Hero` death on the same Tile as a `üè∞ Settlement`, those `üßü Monster` Tokens are placed on the **same Tile** as a `üè∞ Settlement`.  Functionally, this means that the `üßü Monster` Token must be defeated before access to/from the `üè∞ Settlement` is possible (for any `üßô‚Äç Hero`--`Allied` or `Enemy`).  This `üßü Monster` Token is treated like any other `üßü Monster` Token and all normal `üßü Monster` Token rules apply.*
---

## Mines [ ‚öíÔ∏è ]
Upon landing on a tile with a `‚öíÔ∏è Mine` Token, the Player can attempt to capture the `‚öíÔ∏è Mine`.  Upon a successful capture, immediately collect **`üí∞(+1)`**.  Any time a Player attempts to change possession of a `‚öíÔ∏è Mine`, roll a `üé≤ 1dP` to determine if the `‚öíÔ∏è Mine` is guarded by `Guardians`.

If the `üé≤ 1dP` roll results in `Guardians`, the Player must roll (based on the table below) to determine how many `Guardians` will attempt to defend the `‚öíÔ∏è Mine`.  If the Player then defeats the `Guardians`, s/he captures the `‚öíÔ∏è Mine`.

If the `‚öíÔ∏è Mine` was owned by a Player, then no ***bonus*** `üí∞ Resource` is collected for `Guardian` kills, ***only*** a capture and `üí∞(+1)` for the capture.

**Check for Guardians**

|Roll|üé≤[10 - 80]|üé≤[90 - 100]|
|---|---|---|
|üé≤ `1dP`|`CAPTURE`|`FIGHT üßü Monsters`|

**Determine Guardian Quantity**

|Highest Overall|Roll|

|*Villa*|üé≤ `1d6 +2`|
|*Estate*|üé≤ `2d6 +3`|
|*Castle*|üé≤ `3d6 +4`|

**A `‚öíÔ∏è Mine's` `üßü Monster` quantity is determined by whatever Player has the ***highest*** `üè∞ Settlement` rank**

## Items [ üéÅ ]
All Players must agree on a ruleset below, though the Players are allowed to change the ruleset at any point, if it passes a `Vote`.

### Ruleset 1) Card & Dice Rules (Default Ruleset)
Upon landing on a Tile with an `üéÅ Item` Token, draw a card from the `Item Deck`.  Roll the dice stated on the card to determine the `Rarity` of the `üéÅ Item`.  Place a marker on the `Rarity` placeholder on the card's edge.

In lieu of cards, you may instead roll a `üé≤ 1dP` to determine the `Item Type` according to table below.  After determining `Item Type`, roll a `üé≤ 1dP` to determine `Rarity`.  Upon a **Success**, you may roll again; upon a **Failure** *do not* continue to roll.  **Always** start at `Common` and attempt upgrades from there.

**Determine Item Type**

|Roll|üé≤[10 - 70]|üé≤[80 - 100]|

|üé≤ `1dP`|**Failure**|**Success**|

If a Player rolls a **Success**, add `+1 Rarity Level` to the `üéÅ Item`, else remain at current `Rarity Level`.

## Monsters [ üßü ]
Upon landing on a Tile with a `üßü Monster` Token, roll a `üé≤ 1dP` to determine the result, based on the table below.

In the rare event that a Tile has multiple `üßü Monster` Tokens present, you must **aggregate** all rolls into one (cf. **"Multiply-Occupied Tiles"** for routine with example).  In that case, peform actions on the `üßü Monsters` in the follow priority order:

1. Any `DEFECT` rolls that resulted
2. Any `BRIBE` rolls that resulted
3. Any `HARD-FIGHT`/`FIGHT` rolls that resulted

> **IMPORTANT** `üßü Monsters` and `ü§∫ Mercenaries` are the *same*, and as such, any `BRIBE` or `DEFECT` treats them as identical.  The verbiage difference simply reflects where you are likely to encounter them and their general attitude toward you as the Player.

**Roll for Interaction**

|Roll|üé≤[10]|üé≤[20 - 70]|üé≤[80 - 90]|üé≤[100]|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|üé≤ `1dP`|`HARD-FIGHT`|`FIGHT`|`BRIBE`|`DEFECT`|

**Roll for Monster Quantity**

|Situation|Roll|
|---|---|
|`HARD-FIGHT`|`üé≤ 4d6`|
|`FIGHT`|`üé≤ 2d6`|
|`BRIBE`|`üé≤ 2d6`|
|`DEFECT`|`üé≤ 1d6 +3`|

**Cost Implications**

|Situation|Cost `/üßü Monster`|
|---|---|
|`BRIBE`|`3 üí∞`|
|`DEFECT`|`0 üí∞` (Free)|

#### `HARD-FIGHT`/`FIGHT`
`üé≤ 4d6`/`üé≤ 2d6` `üßü Monsters` (depending on `üé≤ 1dP` roll above) `ENGAGE` the `üßô‚Äç Hero`, forcing it to attack.  If the `üßô‚Äç Hero` dies as a result, normal **Hero Death** rules apply.  If the Player wins, however, s/he is awarded the ***integer*** portion of `+üí∞(Monster Quantity / 2)` (i.e. **truncate** the decimal, **do not** round).

> *Example*
> 
> |Roll|Result|
> |-|-|
> |`üé≤ 4d6`|`üé≤[19]`|
> 
> **The Player will fight `üßü(x19)` and, if victorious, will receive `19 / 2 = 9.5` => `üí∞(+9)`**

#### `BRIBE`
Up to `üé≤ 2d6` `üßü Monsters` decide to adventure with the `üßô‚Äç Hero`, at the expense of `üí∞(-3) per üßü Monster` to become (up to) `ü§∫(2d6)` for that `üßô‚Äç Hero`.  The Player does not have to take all of them.

#### `DEFECT`
Up to `üé≤ 1d6 +3` `üßü Monsters` decide to adventure with the `üßô‚Äç Hero` to become `ü§∫(1d6 +3)` for that `üßô‚Äç Hero`, **for FREE**.  The Player does not have to take all of them.

---
- **`üí° REMINDER`** *Simplified, multiple `üßü Monsters` on a single Tile basically treats the Tile as having a much large band of `üßü Monsters`*

### Garrisoned Heroes
A `Garrisoned` `üßô‚Äç Hero` cannot be attacked directly, as it is protected by the `üè∞ Settlement`.  If a `üè∞ Settlement` is attacked while a `üßô‚Äç Hero` is `Garrisoned`, then the `üßô‚Äç Hero` and its `Army` will assist in defending the `üè∞ Settlement`.

> **NOTE:** If A `Garrisoned` `üßô‚Äç Hero` is at a `üè∞ Settlement` while it is attacked, there would be a `üßô‚Äç Hero`, a `üè∞ Settlement`, and the aggregation of the `Recruits` between the `üè∞ Settlement` and the `üßô‚Äç Hero` (if it has any) defending the  `üè∞ Settlement`.
> 
---
- **`üí° REMINDER`** *While a `üßô‚Äç Hero` is `Garrisoned`, `ü§∫ Mercenaries` and `üéÅ Items` can be freely exchanged between the `üè∞ Settlement` and the `üßô‚Äç Hero`*
---

### Armory
A `üè∞ Settlement's` `Armory` is simply a collection of `üéÅ Items` that any `üßô‚Äç Hero` currently `Garrisoned` at that `üè∞ Settlement` can exchange, deposit, or withdraw `üéÅ Items`.

`üéÅ Items` in the `Armory` do not apply to `Battle` rolls in the event of a `üè∞ Settlement` assault.

---
- **`üí° REMINDER`** *The `Armory` is not a building that you can build, every `üè∞ Settlement` comes with it*
---

### Successful Settlement Assault
If a `üè∞ Settlement` falls to an assault, ***all*** `üéÅ Items` in the `Armory` become the property of the conquering party.

> **IMPORTANT** If a `üè∞ Settlement` has any `üéÅ Items` in the `Armory` at the time of a conquest, *those items* are **not** "tokenized" like they would be in the death of a  `üßô‚Äç Hero`--they remain as the cards they are.

If a `üßô‚Äç Hero` was also *defending* a `üè∞ Settlement` in the assault, ***all*** `üéÅ Items` in the Armor and all `üí∞ Resources` at that `üè∞ Settlement` now become the property of the conquering party.

All `üéÅ Items` transferred from the assault go into the `üè∞ Settlement's` `Armory` by default.  While it can be readily transferred to a `Garrisoned` `üßô‚Äç Hero`, conquered `üéÅ Items` default to the `üè∞ Settlement`.

## Multiply-Occupied Tiles
Any Token interaction will cost a minimum of `üêé(-1)`.  This applies to consecutive Token acquisitions on the same Tile.

> **IMPORTANT** `üéÅ Items` and `üí∞ Resources` uniquely take `üêé(-1)` for **each** Token on a Tile, while all other Token are "automatically" interacted with simply by being on the same Tile

If a Tile is occupied by more than `1` Token, then the following priority order ***must*** be followed ***before*** a `üßô‚Äç Hero` can [ `INTERACT`, `ENGAGE` ] with lower-priority Tokens:

|Priority|Token(s)|Notes|
|-|-|-|
|1|`üßü Monster`|-|
|2|`üßô‚Äç Hero`*|See `üí° REMINDER` below|
|3|`üè∞ Settlement`|-|
|4|`üéÅ Item` `üí∞ Resource`|Player's choice|
|5|`‚öíÔ∏è Mine`|-|

In the rare event that a Tile has multiple `üßü Monster` Tokens present, you must **aggregate** all rolls into one.  In that case, peform actions on the `üßü Monsters` in the follow priority order:

1. Any `DEFECT` rolls that resulted
2. Any `BRIBE` rolls that resulted
3. Any `HARD-FIGHT`/`FIGHT` rolls that resulted

---
- **`üí° REMINDER`** *`üßô‚Äç Heroes` `Garrisoned` in a `üè∞ Settlement` are considered a `üè∞ Settlement` by priority standards, and become another unit on the `Settlement's` `Battlefield`*
---

## Miscellaneous

### Dice
As a notational explicative for this section and the guide in general:

* `üé≤[#]` will mean a roll ***result***
* `üé≤[#] +#` will mean a roll ***result*** that must have a `B` "Bonus" added to get the total ***after all dice have been rolled***
* `üé≤[#]` + `...` + `üé≤[#]` will mean a roll ***result*** sequence that resulted from multiple rolls
* `üé≤ NdS +B` will mean a dice roll ***to perform***
* `üé≤ NdS +B` + `...` + `üé≤ NdS +B` will mean a dice roll sequence ***to perform***
* `(# * üé≤ NdS) +B` + `...` + `(# * üé≤ NdS) +B` will mean a dice roll sequence ***to perform*** of **Representative Rolls**
* `(# * üé≤ NdS) +B` will mean a dice roll ***to perform***, the result of which will be multiplied by `#` and `+B` will be *added to the final result*.  This is called a **Representative Roll**

#### Bonus
Bonus is **always** added *at the very end* of ***all*** rolls.  An example roll of `üé≤ 2d6 +9` means:

---
> **‚úîÔ∏è CORRECT ‚úîÔ∏è**
> 
> This is a *correct* method of calculation (i.e. **Individual Rolls**)
> 
> * `üé≤ 2d6 +9` is consistent with (`üé≤ 1d6` + `üé≤ 1d6` + `+9`)
---
> **‚úîÔ∏è CORRECT ‚úîÔ∏è**
> 
> This is a *correct* method of calculation (i.e. **Representative Rolls**)
> If you remember PEMDAS from math, read `# * üé≤ NdS +B` and `(# * üé≤ NdS) +B` both as `(# * üé≤ NdS) +B`
> 
> To be representative, you would only roll `1` `üé≤ d6` and *multiply* that result by `N` (in the case below, `N` is `2`).

> * `üé≤ 2d6 +9` is consistent with (`(2 * üé≤ 1d6)` + `+9`)
---
> **‚ùå WRONG ‚ùå**

#### Number-Sided-Bonus (`üé≤ NdS +B`)
When writing dice rolls, the "Number-Sided-Bonus" convention is used, taking the general form of `üé≤ NdS +B`.
* `N` is the "Number"
  - The *quantity* of dice
* `S` is the "Sidedness"
  - The *type* of dice
* `B` is the "Bonus"
  - After rolling all of the dice, *add* the bonus

> *Example*
> 
> A `üé≤ 3d6 +9` means that we will
> * **Firstly**, Roll a `6-sided` dice `3` times
>   - e.g. `üé≤ 3d6 +9` => `üé≤[4]` + `üé≤[2]` + `üé≤[6]` = `üé≤[12]`
> * **Lastly**, Sum the total and then *add* `+9` to it
>   - e.g. `üé≤[12]` + `+9` = `21`

|Dice|Sides|Notes|
|-|-|-|
|`üé≤ d4`|`4`|Increments of `1` from `üé≤[1 - 4]`|
|`üé≤ d6`|`6`|Increments of `1` from `üé≤[1 - 6]`|
|`üé≤ d10`|`10`|Increments of `1` from `üé≤[1 - 10]`|
|`üé≤ d12`|`12`|Increments of `1` from `üé≤[1 - 12]`|
|`üé≤ dP`|`10`|Increments of `10` from `üé≤[10 - 00]`,  `üé≤[00]` = `100%`.|

#### Dice Arithmetic

> **PSA**
> 
> Unless you understand this deeply, we recommend that you **do not** attempt to condense dice if they differ in their "Sidedness" (e.g. `üé≤ 1d4 +9` + `üé≤ 3d12 +4` + `üé≤ 2d6` + `üé≤ 1d6` + ..., etc.), but instead simply roll each dice in sequence and keep track of the running total.
> 
> In cases like the above, we recommend that you simply roll each in sequence and write down a running total and only perform condensing on `üé≤ d6` dice roll sequences.  Because of this simplification, we intentionally attempted to keep the majority of dice rolls in the paradigm of `üé≤ d6` for actions, and `üé≤ dP` for chances.
 
That being said, if you have multiple of the *same* **Sidedness** on the combined dice roll, you can condense them into a single expression in a fairly straightforward manner.

* Firstly, simply add all the "Numbers" together as the final `N`
* Next, add all of the `B` "Bonuses" together into a final `+B`
* Lastly, consolidate into a single form: `NdS +B`, where `S` is whatever the "Sidedness" of the original dice were.

> **ASSUME:** You have `2d6 +6`, `1d6 +3`, `1d6`, `1d6`.  In this case, since we are only using `üé≤ d6` dice, we can consolidate.
> 
> |Roll||Running Total of `N`|Running Total of `+B`|
> |-|-|-|-|
> |`üé≤ 2d6 +6`|-|`{2} = 2`|`{6} = 6`|
> |`üé≤ 1d6 +3`|-|`2 + {1} = 3`|`6 + {3} = 9`|
> |`üé≤ 1d6`|-|`2 + 1 + {1} = 4`|`6 + 3 = 9`|
> |`üé≤ 1d6`|-|`2 + 1 + 1 + {1} = 5`|`6 + 3 = 9`|
> 
> Take
> 
> * the final `N` of `= 5`
> * the final `B` of `= 9`
> * and the `S` of `üé≤ d6` from the example's initial setup
> 
> into a consolidated form of: `üé≤ 5d6 +9`.

### Voting
Do not read this section too rigidly, it is meant as a high-level guidance, but the ultimate decision is up to you.

If a situation arises where a `Vote` is necessary, then each Player can "cast" `1 Vote`.  In the event of a tie (e.g. an even number of Players), choose a reconciliation method that has fair and even odds (e.g. flip a coin, roll a `1dP` and split `10 - 50` and `60 - 100`, etc.), or create a fun and/or competitive event to decide.

> **TIP:** In the event of a tie, consider introducing light-hearted fun at the other Players' expense, such as pitting each side of the `Vote` against each other by some means (e.g. a mini event, a trivia contest, etc.).

### Fractional Remains
Intuitively, imagine all fractional roles as if they were dividing people and you must keep them alive--you can't have half a person (at least, not a person that is still alive...).

Any time an event happens where there are fractional remains, you must ***always remove (truncate) the decimal portion***.  This remains true *even if* the fraction would *round* up (e.g. 0.6).

### Alliance
An `Alliance` can be made with any Player at any time, and if an `Alliance` forms, then each Player continues to operate independently; however, each Player in the `Alliance` can treat the other `Alliance Members'` `üè∞ Settlements` and `Mines` and **unoccupied Tiles** for movement.

*Any other perks or restrictions are determined by the `Alliance Members` themselves.*

Alliance business is a good situation to introduce `Voting`, if the number of Players warrants it.
